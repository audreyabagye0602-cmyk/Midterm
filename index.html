Montefiore Plaza Redesign

Reimagining urban space for safety, comfort, and community engagement.

About the Project

Creating a better, safer plaza is essential because it directly impacts how people experience and use public space in their daily lives. A well-designed plaza encourages walking, social interaction, and community engagement, while reducing risks from traffic, poor lighting, and overcrowding. When safety is prioritized through clear pathways, visibility, and thoughtful design, people of all ages—especially children, seniors, and those with disabilities—feel more comfortable spending time there. Safer plazas also support local businesses by attracting more visitors and creating a vibrant, active environment. Beyond functionality, an improved plaza can foster a stronger sense of community identity and belonging, transforming an underused or unsafe area into a welcoming space where people gather, relax, and connect. .

The Problem

Lack of Safety

Poor lighting and unclear pedestrian zones create unsafe conditions.

Low Engagement

The plaza lacks seating, activities, and community interaction.

Poor Design

Hard surfaces and minimal greenery reduce comfort and usability.
