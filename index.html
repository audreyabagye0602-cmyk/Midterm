Montefiore Plaza Redesign

Reimagining the Montefiore Plaza space for safety, comfort, and community engagement.

About the Project

Creating a better, safer plaza is essential because it directly impacts how people experience and use public space in their daily lives. A well-designed plaza encourages walking, social interaction, and community engagement, while reducing risks from traffic, poor lighting, and overcrowding. When safety is prioritized through clear pathways, visibility, and thoughtful design, people of all ages—especially children, seniors, and those with disabilities—feel more comfortable spending time there. Safer plazas also support local businesses by attracting more visitors and creating a vibrant, active environment. Beyond functionality, an improved plaza can foster a stronger sense of community identity and belonging, transforming an underused or unsafe area into a welcoming space where people gather, relax, and connect.

The Problem

Lack of Safety

Poor lighting create unsafe conditions during the night time.

Low Engagement

The plaza lacks stores, activities and community interaction.

Poor Design

Hard surfaces and minimal flashy greenery reduce comfort and usability.

Site / Context

Montefiore Plaza currently feels low in engagement and lacks the elements needed to attract and hold people’s attention. Several surrounding buildings have out-of-business storefronts, which contributes to a sense of inactivity and neglect in the area. Although there is a significant amount of grass, the landscape is not visually appealing due to the absence of more vibrant plantings such as flowers or diverse greenery. In addition, the nearby train station remains unfinished, leaving the space surrounded by fences that create both physical and visual barriers. Altogether, these factors make the plaza feel minimalistic and underdeveloped, limiting its potential as a lively and welcoming public space.
